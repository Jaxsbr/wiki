🔴 13 AI-Slop Tells — Annotated

  1. Dark-mode default — AI reaches for dark backgrounds without being asked, because dark SaaS sites dominated 2023–24 training data
  2. "VibeCode Purple" gradient — lavender-to-blue hero gradient is the single most cited AI tell; overrepresented in training data from that era
  3. Decorative floating orbs — blurred gradient circles with no informational purpose; fill space with visual noise
  4. Badge above headline — small pill reading "AI-Powered", "Trusted by 10k+", or "New Feature"; signals template origin
  5. Vague headline with em-dash — "Build the Future—Without Limits"; nothing pinned to what the product actually does
  6. Gradient text — colour transitions applied to headlines for spectacle without meaning; purely decorative
  7. Generic CTAs — "Get Started" and "Learn More" appear on virtually every AI-generated page; interchangeable across industries
  8. Tiny ALL-CAPS label before heading — "WHY CHOOSE US" directly above the section heading; a layout tic with no user benefit
  9. Three-column feature card grid — the canonical AI layout; appears regardless of whether 3 columns is appropriate
  10. Uniform 16px border radius — applied to every card, button, and container; creates visual monotony
  11. Coloured left border on cards — 3–4px purple/blue stripe; called "almost as reliable a tell as em-dashes are in copy"
  12. Glassmorphism + uniform 24px padding — frosted-glass effect on every card; identical padding eliminates hierarchy
  13. Icon floating above heading — emoji or line-icon placed above the card title, not integrated with content
Plus the unlabelled honourable mentions: generic SaaS copy ("streamline / empower / world-class / cutting-edge"), stats rows with round numbers, testimonials with stock initials-avatars, Inter font throughout (loaded at top of this page). Designers report identifying this constellation in ~0.5 seconds.
